About Us

Founded in 1861 by Hungarian brothers Raul and Fergusan Barabas, Sombrearo started as a humble venture delivering goods across the Wild West. The company's logo - a stylized bear donning a sombrero and an eyepatch - reflected their rugged and resourceful approach to business. In those early days, Sombrearo the Barabos brothers Raul and Ferg were producing hand crafted eyepatches. As Raul once fought in a duel where he lost an eye.

As the railroads began to crisscross the country, however, Sombrearo's fortunes started to decline. The brothers found themselves struggling to compete with the efficiency and scale of the new transportation networks. It was a bitter pill to swallow, but Raul and Fergusan blamed their misfortune on an unexpected culprit: the Chinese. "Those Orientals thought they could outdo us at every turn," Raul would often grumble, his thick Hungarian accent dripping with disdain. The Barabas brothers' animosity towards the Chinese was rooted in a deep-seated mistrust of their business acumen and perceived unfair competition.

Despite this setback, Sombrearo persevered by diversifying its offerings. In the early days, they had sold water divination sticks to prospectors and cowpokes seeking guidance on the treacherous trails. As the market shifted, they adapted, offering bespoke saddles, custom-made rifles, and even exotic leather goods.
